# Module 2: Common Cyber Threats

## Learning Objectives

- Recognize major categories of cyber threats.
- Understand how attacks usually begin.
- Learn practical ways to reduce risk.

## Common Threat Types

### 1) Malware

Malicious software such as viruses, worms, ransomware, and spyware.

### 2) Phishing

Fraudulent messages designed to trick users into revealing credentials or clicking malicious links.

### 3) Password Attacks

Brute force, credential stuffing, and password spraying target weak/reused passwords.

### 4) Social Engineering

Manipulating people into bypassing security procedures.

### 5) Denial of Service (DoS/DDoS)

Overwhelming a service with traffic to make it unavailable.

## Typical Attack Chain

1. Reconnaissance
2. Initial access (phishing, exposed service, stolen credentials)
3. Privilege escalation
4. Lateral movement
5. Data theft or disruption

## Prevention Basics

- Keep software and OS patched.
- Use MFA.
- Verify emails and links before clicking.
- Back up critical data.
- Apply least-privilege access.

## Mini Scenario

An attacker sends an invoice email with a fake login page. A user enters credentials. Attackers then access email and internal documents.

**Defense:** phishing training, MFA, and suspicious-login alerts.

## Summary

Most incidents start with simple weaknesses: unpatched systems, weak credentials, or human error.
