# Module 1: Introduction to Cybersecurity

## Learning Objectives

- Define cybersecurity in simple terms.
- Understand why cybersecurity matters to individuals and organizations.
- Identify common assets that require protection.

## What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, applications, and data from unauthorized access, disruption, theft, and damage.

It combines:

- **People** (awareness and behavior)
- **Processes** (policies and incident response)
- **Technology** (tools such as firewalls, MFA, and endpoint protection)

## Why It Matters

Modern life depends on digital systems. Cyber incidents can cause:

- Financial loss
- Service outages
- Data leaks and privacy violations
- Reputation damage

## Core Assets to Protect

- Personal data (names, IDs, emails)
- Credentials (passwords, tokens, API keys)
- Business systems (servers, applications)
- Intellectual property (source code, documents)

## Real-World Example

A company employee reuses a weak password. Attackers obtain it from a previous breach, log in, and access internal systems. This can be prevented with strong passwords and multi-factor authentication.

## Quick Check

1. Cybersecurity is only for large companies. **(False)**
2. People and process are part of cybersecurity, not only tools. **(True)**

## Summary

Cybersecurity protects digital assets through a combination of human awareness, structured process, and technical controls.
